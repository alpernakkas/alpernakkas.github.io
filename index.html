My work sits at the intersection of landscape ecology, cultural memory, and historical narratives of the natural world. I examine how human communities have understood, shaped, and been shaped by their environments across centuries.

Areas of Focus

Research Interests

· · ·
🌍
Environmental Humanities

Interdisciplinary investigation of human relationships with the natural world through cultural, historical, and philosophical lenses.

📜
Historical Ecology

Reconstructing past landscapes and ecological regimes using archival records, oral histories, and paleoecological data.

🏡
Place & Memory

How communities encode meaning into landscapes, and how those meanings shift across generational time and political change.
